2016 Election Map

The United States 2016 presidential election seemed like it would be an easy victory for the DNC's Hillary Clinton over the GOP's Donald Trump. However, Trump came out the victor, depsite having three million less votes than his opponent. This election has inspired me to dig deeper into the voting data and explore various relationships through almost every county across the nation. Through this investigation, I also wanted to become more familiar with choropleth maps in R, and turn them into interactive maps using the leaflet package.. If you would like to see the code used to make this, click here.

Examination Through Mapping

There are a few different ways to explore the map. Clicking on an individual county reveals relevant election data about that specific county, such as the county name, the winnnig candidate, the number of votes each candidate received, and the voting margin between them. The radio buttons in the bottom left allow the map to be shaded in four different way. The first map examines the candidate who won each county, where Clinton is mapped to blue counties and Trump to red. The next map shows the percent margin of each county using a diverging color palette, where the darker the shade of the county, the larger the percent margin is for that candidate. The third and fourth maps are similar to the second, except these show vote margins by candidate.

Creation of the Map

Making this map was actually quite a challenge for me. As it turns out, I ended up using three different datasets combined into one larger dataset. One dataset had general party election information by county, another had candidate data by county, and the final was a shapefile to use during the actual creation of the map. The major downfall of this data is that some counties were excluded, so if the candidate data set was missing one county, it had to be removed from the other too. This is why Washington D.C. and the entire state of Alaska were removed from the final map.

Once the data was compiled it was smooth sailing. All I had to complete was the palettes, popup, and the map itself. It's worth mentioning that the diverging color palette used in the second map had to be crafted carefully, due to the fact that the color scales darker with larger winning margins. In order to represent the margins accurately, the palettes must scale equally both ways since the two candidates' maximum are different sizes.
