2016 Presidential Election Analysis

The United States 2016 presidential election seemed like it would be an easy victory for the DNC's Hillary Clinton over the GOP's Donald Trump. However, Trump came out the victor, depsite having 3,000,000 less votes than his opponent. This election has inspired me to dig deeper into the voting data and explore various relationships through almost every county across the nation. Through this investigation, I also wanted to become even more familiar with R, and learn how to create interactive and user-friendly maps. If you would like to see the code used to make this, click here. So without further ado, here is my most recent iteration of the map!

Examination Through Mapping

There are a few different ways to explore the map. Clicking on an individual county reveals relevant election data to that specific county. The radio buttons in the bottom left allow the user to select one of four maps to view. The first map examines the candidate who won each county, where Clinton is mapped to blue counties and Trump to red. The next map shows the percent margin of each county. The darker the shade of the county, the bigger the percent margin is for that candidate. The third and fourth maps are similar to the second, except these show vote margins by candidate.
